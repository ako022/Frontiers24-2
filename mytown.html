Ako's Page - My Town

Welcome to the My Town page! Here you can learn a little bit more about Worcester, the home of the Frontiers program.

Worcester's story begins in the 1800s, when it developed largely as an industrial city. It is now the second-most populous city in Massachusetts, after Boston. Modern-day Worcester is known for its diverse population and as a center of higher education. 22% of Worcester's residents were born outside of the United States! It is also home to eight colleges and universities, including the one and only Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI), where the Frontiers program is hosted.

Founded in 1865, WPI began as the United States' first STEM schools. It now boasts 14 different academic departments with over 50 undergraduate and graduate degree programs spanning both STEM and humanities and the arts.

WPI's curriculum is focused on project-based learning, where students are able to engage with their subject material in a more tangible way than at other colleges.

Outside of WPI and its many other educational facilities, Worcester is also home to a number of art associations.The Worcester Music Festival, New England Metal and Hardcore Festival, and stART on the Street are all annual art and music festivals hosted in Worcester.
